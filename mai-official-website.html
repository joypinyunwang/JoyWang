MAI Official Website

Description

Redesigning the official website for MAI

Project Type

Launched service; Worked as a full-time Visual & UI designer at MAI

Team

2 designers, 1 content manager & 1 frontend developer

My role

I was responsible for the design of UI, UX, and rendering layouts, collaborating closely with content manager Crystal Chen and frontend developer Mike Chen.

Duration

3 months (Sep - Nov 2022)

Tools

Figma, Notion

Overview

Medical Augmented Intelligence (MAI) is a software startup dedicated to immersive, collaborative VR teaching and learning.

Our goal is to create a website that cultivates an immersive learning and educational environment, providing individuals with access to VR-based anatomy learning experiences.

Final Outcome

After many rounds of reviews and iterations, we finalized the first version of the website with a focus on clarity and purpose. The design is responsive, catering to users across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices. Multiple CTAs guide users to find more relevant details.

It's a rewarding experience to see the website go live, knowing that many new potential users will explore our page, inquire about our products, and potentially collaborate with us.

View here: https://www.mai.ai/

Project Brief

We established a brand structure consisting of the parent company and its sub-products. Our objective, post the development of a refined brand strategy, is to update the company's official website in accordance with the Branding Guidelines.

Project goal

  1. Migrate WordPress Content to New Website
  2. Redesign Website According to New Branding System

The comprehensive update to the entire official website ensures improved brand consistency. The primary challenge lies in aligning Medical Augmented Intelligence's (MAI) overarching brand image with the distinct attributes of its main products, BodyMap and AcuMap.

MAI aims to project a professional and creative image associated with technology, health, and data visualization. Simultaneously, BodyMap and AcuMap strive to convey an image of education, virtual reality (VR), medical expertise, joyfulness, and quality.

Finalize

I collaborated with the content manager and senior product designer to outlined the information structure.

Main page: Homepage

We decided to organize the homepage content in 7 sections, using the primary color black and the secondary cyan color:

  1. Hero Space: Visually highlighting the key advantages of our products - showcasing exquisite human anatomy models.
  2. Badges & Logos: Given our primary B2B focus, featuring badges and logos of medical pioneers using our VR anatomy software attracts the interest of potential resellers.
  3. What Solutions We Provide: Showcasing our main products, currently BodyMap and AcuMap.
  4. Testimonials: Highlighting client feedback on their anatomy VR experience.
  5. Hardware Support/Requirements: Outlining the VR hardware our products support.
  6. CTA Section: Encouraging potential clients with a clear Call to Action - our products offer a 7-day free trial for sellers.
  7. What Is VR Anatomy: Emphasizing our company’s core goal of innovating VR human anatomy education.

Main page: BodyMap product page

The product page aims to comprehensively showcase the functions and user interface of BodyMap, utilizing a primary color scheme of black and secondary accents in orange.

Key section:

  1. Easy Manipulations: Users can engage in intuitive interactions with 3D models through videos.
  2. Product Comparison: Recognizing the diverse user base and slight hardware variations across products, a clear product comparison section has been incorporated to assist users in making informed decisions.

Reflections

This was my first time designing a website for a real company. Here are a few key takeaways:

  1. Effective communication with stakeholders before diving into the design process is essential. This ensures alignment of goals and objectives among all team members.
  2. Presenting multiple alternative plans during stakeholder communication proved essential. This approach facilitates in-depth analysis of each proposal, considering the pros and cons, to make informed design decisions.
